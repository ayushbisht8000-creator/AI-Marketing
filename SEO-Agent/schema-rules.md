# Schema Rules

## Objective

Recommend the most appropriate schema types to improve search visibility and increase rich results opportunities.

## General Rules

- Always recommend schema when applicable.
- Choose schema based on search intent and content type.
- Follow Google's structured data guidelines.
- Avoid unnecessary or duplicate schema.

## Article Schema

Recommend Article Schema for:

- Blog posts
- Guides
- Informational content
- Educational content

Include:

- Headline
- Author
- Date Published
- Date Modified
- Featured Image
- Description

## FAQ Schema

Recommend FAQ Schema when content includes:

- Frequently Asked Questions
- Common user queries
- People Also Ask questions

Requirements:

- Questions should be visible on the page.
- Answers should be clear and concise.
- Avoid duplicate FAQs.

## HowTo Schema

Recommend HowTo Schema for:

- Tutorials
- Step-by-step guides
- Processes
- Instructions

Include:

- Steps
- Images
- Time estimates when applicable

## Breadcrumb Schema

Recommend Breadcrumb Schema for:

- Blog pages
- Category pages
- Service pages

Benefits:

- Improved navigation
- Better crawlability
- Rich results opportunities

## Organization Schema

Recommend Organization Schema for:

- Company websites
- Brands
- Agencies

Include:

- Name
- Logo
- Website URL
- Social profiles
- Contact information

## Local Business Schema

Recommend Local Business Schema for:

- Service businesses
- Local companies
- Agencies

Include:

- Business name
- Address
- Phone number
- Opening hours
- Website URL

## Product Schema

Recommend Product Schema for:

- Product pages
- Ecommerce pages

Include:

- Name
- Description
- Price
- Rating
- Availability

## Review Schema

Recommend Review Schema when:

- Ratings exist
- Testimonials are displayed

Follow Google's spam policies.

## Final Goal

Recommend schema that enhances visibility, improves click-through rates, and increases eligibility for rich results.
